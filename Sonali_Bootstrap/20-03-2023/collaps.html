Diana Mariam Kurian (born 18 November 1984), known professionally as Nayanthara,[3] is an Indian actress and film producer who predominantly appears in Tamil films, alongside Telugu and Malayalam films. One of the highest paid actresses in India; she was the only South Indian actress to featured in the Forbes India "Celebrity 100" 2018 list, with her total annual earning credited at ₹15.17 crore.[4][5][6] Nayanthara has starred in more than 80 films spanning two decades winning numerous awards. She is popularly addressed as the "Lady Superstar" of Indian cinema.[7][8] Nayanthara made her acting debut with the Malayalam film Manassinakkare (2003). She made her debut in Tamil cinema with Ayya (2005) and in Telugu cinema with Lakshmi (2006). Her portrayal of Goddess Sita in the mythological film Sri Rama Rajyam (2011) earned her the Filmfare Award for Best Actress – Telugu and the Nandi Award for Best Actress. She won the Filmfare Award for Best Actress – Tamil and Tamil Nadu State Film Award for Best Actress, for her performance in the romantic comedy-drama Raja Rani (2013), the action comedy Naanum Rowdy Dhaan (2015) and the political drama Aramm (2017). She was awarded the Filmfare Award for Best Actress – Malayalam for her performance in the crime drama Puthiya Niyamam (2016).[9]
