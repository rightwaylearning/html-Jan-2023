India
In the early 16th century, northern India, then under mainly Muslim rulers,[121] fell again to the superior mobility and firepower of a new generation of Central Asian warriors.[122] The resulting Mughal Empire did not stamp out the local societies it came to rule. Instead, it balanced and pacified them through new administrative practices[123][124] and diverse and inclusive ruling elites,[125] leading to more systematic, centralised, and uniform rule
